  • Intern, Hangzhou, China

    Summer, 2014

    In the summer of 2014, I applied for an internship and participated in practical work experiences with the State Grid Tonglu Electric Power Co., Ltd. This is where I interacted with practical operation of the dispatching communication, relay protection, monitor reception for power grid in the summer. During the internship, I worked with operators in the monitor and dispatching center adjusting threshold values of detection systems and attended a drill regarding potential electric issues in order to deal with summer emergencies. Additionally, I wrote reviews of a program that aims at preventing emergencies during the summer months when the electrical power is especially in need. The program includes a series of examinations and maintenance of transmission lines, transformers, and generators.

  • Hangzhou

    1993-2008

    I was born and raised in Hangzhou until leaving for college. There is a saying, "under the heaven there is Suzhou and Hangzhou", which indicates that Hangzhou is reckoned a beautiful city long time ago.

    The West Lake is fantastic, not only are all parks free to visitors, one can almost always find places with stunning beauty and peace around the Lake and the surrounding hiking trails-all within less than 30min from the city center, easily accessible by car, bus, bikes and on foot. The Lake and the hiking trails make weekend getaways so easy. If you are lucky enough to live close by the Lake, the you have the perfect morning run routes (10k for a big loop).

    People in Hangzhou have a slower pace of life comparing to tier-1 cities like Beijing, Shanghai, and Shenzhen. Growing up it feels like I spent the majority of my weekends with my family members biking somewhere around the Lake.
